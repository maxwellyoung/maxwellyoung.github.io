Problem Solving

06.06.22

Doing the whereAreYou problem I was stuck. To solve the problem you have to write a function, which is simply a reusable piece of code that you give a name that is given an array of elements and finds in each array which door Scooby is hiding behind. I managed to use a built in function called find to find Scooby in each array and save these instances to a variable (which is basically a box which I put all the found Scoobys inside) but then was confused how this corresponded to me finding the door number. I knew dot notation (which is where you can specify children of an object, such as the Scooby object I’d made) would be used on an object above the position the door number was stored in the hierarchy, but accessing it in the way I expected wasn’t working. The error message I was receiving repeatedly read that the object I’d saved the found Scoobys into was “undefined”. At this point I tried many reconfigurations of the code, did some googling and still couldn’t get anywhere. I was feeling quite defeated by how I was befuddled by such a small piece of code and doubting myself. Of course I knew I wouldn’t give up and I still had options available to me. This kind of confusion is natural having just learnt these concepts of programming. I decided to do both of the two things I thought could help me in such a stuck, frustrated state. I asked for help on the Dev Academy Discord with a snippet of my code and took a break from the question to centre myself with mindfulness activities.

When I returned the help I’d gotten wasn’t working out. “I already tried this”, I’d thought. The course facilitator suggested it was an issue with function scope. I’ve learnt a bit of how important scope and am aware it’s a common reason for code errors but I still was getting the same result of my found Scooby box/object being undefined. I should’ve mentioned earlier but undefined in code means it has no qualities to it, nothing inside it, no values or properties, zilch! Well, 0 is quite different from undefined in code actually, but that’s another matter. Still feeling frustrated I took did some further research on built-in array methods to see if I was missing anything glaringly obvious, couldn’t find anything so I took a walk. On the walk I saw the facilitator had sent another message saying the previous scope suggestion was leading me down the wrong path and it was more a matter of there being an empty array that my function was checking for, an array without a door number, Scooby or anyone else for that matter! This was extremely helpful and I sighed in relief thinking this was something I could manage on my own to amend in my function. I added a conditional statement that checked for if the found Scooby object/box was undefined and made of those instances return 0, the rest would have a door number and return with that very number. I was overjoyed that this programming stuff wasn’t as beyond me as I was beginning to think, but there was still one to go..

Research Neuroplasticity

Discuss how understanding the principles of neuroplasticity benefits people.

People used to believe once your brain had developed into an adult brain it was fixed in place. But this isn’t true, it’s constantly adapting and changing to new information with the engagement of learning processes. Every time we think, feel or do pathways in our brain light up. The pathways of things that have become habitual for us will be more ingrained and less difficult. People are encouraged to try new things knowing that we can rewire our brains to get over bad habits and learn new processes through carving new pathways. I wish I was aware the brain worked like this in high school as I imagine it would’ve made me less discouraged comparing myself to others.

Topics that are off-putting like mathematics which always felt uncreative to me can be recontextualised through imagining and by being a means to a creative end. Mathematics is a good example of a topic for Neuroplasticity. The practice of mathematics is entirely imaginary and can be thought of as a trek into an unknown forest. Repetition of certain mental processes or tasks. Research Growth Mindset

Discuss what it is and why it is relevant.

A growth mindset is the belief that our talents can be developed through doing the Mahi, getting help from others around us and considering strategies. Studies show that people that follow this belief achieve a higher ability than those with fixed mindsets, people who people they were born with certain abilities and lack other abilities. When people have this growth mindset people are more eager to embark on the task at hand no matter how challenging. It makes them part of a team working towards a collective goal rather than an isolated person with a list of abilities that can be contributed when required. A growth mindset also would lead you to compare yourself less and avoid a demeaning attitude towards yourself or others.

When you’re stuck with a growth mindset instead of feeling brutally discouraged, maybe to the point of wanting to give up, you feel encouraged to try new approaches or reach out for help. A fixed mindset wrongly makes you think of these as a last resort or failures while they will often lead you to learn more than you would otherwise.

In this exploration, did anything surprise you? Change for you?

It helped me realise the importance of recognising progress! It’s an essential part of learning or working and shouldn’t go unrecognised. On the other side of the coin, I think the benefit of this mode of learning gives you more confidence the more you achieve with it. Once you’ve learnt one skill that felt completely foreign to you you are more open to giving new things a go. The more you solve programming problems the more belief you have that you are growing in your understanding of the skill so you are more likely to embark on the next one with confidence and less likely for moments of getting stuck to halt your progress. It goes beyond saying you appreciate values of growth etc. consistent action aligning with growth mindset thinking must be made and maintained.

How will you integrate a growth mindset into your learning journey?

I’ll try to think of times I surpassed my perceived limit of myself when I am stuck and teetering on feeling programming/problem solving is beyond my ability. I have a lot of anxiety about this right now but knowledge of a growth mindset is making me more eager than fearful (I think!)

An analogy to describe JavaScript and its relationship to HTML and CSS.

The construction of a website can be thought of in similar terms to the construction of a liveable house. The first step to build a house (after the design stage is done) is hire people to construct the entire frame of the structure walls, the floors. This is the foundation of the house. The language of HTML (HyperText Markup Language) is to create what this is for a website. A website has something similar to rooms in a page having different sections with different purposes like how rooms have different purposes. This hierarchy isn’t only visual when implemented correctly in HTML. The addition of semantic HTML allows for visually impaired people who use screen readers to navigate to where they need to get on a website much easier. Once a houses framework is created it needs interior design and decoration. Paint on the walls, flooring, lighting. The decoration of a website is handled via the CSS (Cascading Style Sheets) language.

Control flow is the process in which the machine runs the lines of code, from top to bottom, storing functions repeating loops, conditionals which determine the flow and loops which repeat sections of code. document object model is a tree of web elements. The tree of a website gets very complex as it covers every part that makes up the site. It allows for you to treat HTML/CSS elements similarly to the way you would Javascript objects.

Explain the difference between accessing data from arrays and objects.

To put it simply you use arrays instead of objects when the data you are storing should be in order. The rest of the time you can use objects. Arrays are usually data structures of numbers. Numbers inside an array would become the elements of that array. Objects are better suited for text elements. One reason to use arrays for data rather than objects is for their length property. Arrays are objects but only in javascript. The definitive part of arrays is that they store the data in an ordered list so to access arrays elements you use their numerical index. To delete elements from arrays you use the pop() function while you’d use the delete keyword for deleting object properties.

Functions are helpful because not dissimilar to a recipe, they store simple or complex processes under one name that can be reused. They can be as short or as long as the programmer requires.

Emotional Intelligence

30.05.22

What is emotional intelligence?

Ones natural ability to read the sentiments of themselves and those around them to different situations or scenarios and their instinct of what would help in that state. Empathy for someone else's perspective with all their different experiences and points of view a core part of being emotionally intelligent. It allows us to maintain a certain level of tolerance and bring another level of comprehension to life events with turbulent emotional factors. There is a level of balance to ones well being that emotional intelligence brings. It reaches the emotional subtext of a person, acknowledging people's actions aren’t made in a vacuum but are rather, accustomed to patterns, misinterpreted, possibly entirely unexplainable to you at present but profoundly understandable later.

How is it different to IQ?

The first difference that comes to my mind is that it’s not measured to the extent IQ is or can be. While they are both levels of ability the similarities don’t go much further. IQ is the defined metric of someone's intellect. One could be very successful in their understanding of complex systems or execution of difficult tasks etc but lack key emotional comprehension. Often IQ comes at the expense of other human qualities whether out of ego or neglect. This is being tolerated less and less by groups whether employers or audiences, therefore emotional intelligence should be recognised and worked at as much if not more than IQ.

Why is it important?

It’s important for a plethora of reasons. Perhaps the foremost reason is for your own wellbeing. The balanced approach creates a fortitude in the self. This balanced approach is also helpful in our relationships. Workplaces can be very stressful and often people from a wide variety of backgrounds have different approaches. For me acknowledging the natural ebb and flow of my emotions has always centred me somewhat. Knowing for every down there is an equal up and vice versa. Being less precious about when things go wrong helps us to not feel so crushed as to act out drastically and immaturely.

What are the differences between relative, absolute, and fixed positioning?

18.04.22

Positioning is a key part of CSS web design.

It allows you to change the behaviour of different elements on the page whether text, an image, card etc.

There are in total 5 position types. These are "static", "absolute", "relative", "fixed" & "sticky".

Static is the default or normal position type. It'll keep elements following the normal flow of the site.

position: static;

Absolute can be a useful positioning type for specific placement of things like positioning is probably the strangest of the position types.

position: absolute;

Relative positioning is probably the strangest of the position types.

Without other positioning declarations the element will be treated no different to a static. However when say a bottom: 10px is applied, the bottom it’ll be positioned from will be from the point the element would be without positioning declarations.

Relative will maintain the normal flow of the document. The website CSS-Tricks puts its as “relative to itself”. If you need to define an elements “z-index” and want to remove it from the document flow, use position: absolute. If you need to define an elements “z-index” and want to maintain the document flow, use position: relative.

position: relative;

Fixed positioning works how you might imagine from the title. The viewport serves as the basis for the positioning of a fixed elements. Because the viewport remains the viewport no matter where the user has scrolled or navigated to, fixed elements remain in tthe same defined space, frozen in time & space.. For example the navigation bar on this blog remains at the same position and is constantly visible even when you scroll down to view more blog posts.

position: fixed;

Sticky is a hybrid of relative and fixed positioning. The element sticky positioning is applied to is treated as relative up until it crosses a defined threshold at which point the element becomes fixed, maybe until another threshold is reached. You could think of it as the stacking position. For instance on this blog, the date to the right of the screen is using sticky positioning to keep the date in the same place as you scroll through the blog post until the threshold of the next blog post is reached and the date of that post follows the same behaviour as the previous date.

position: sticky;
